Founder's Note

A note from Jamie Booth

Here’s the honest truth: we’re doing good work. Work we’re proud of. And it’s still not enough.

The scale of need is bigger than any one company can carry. In the Philippines—where much of our work is focused—the gaps are visible every day. Beyond it, there are places where the need is deeper, funding is thinner, and urgency is sharper.

We grow. We hire. We create opportunity. And still, we’re operating on the edges of a much larger problem.

So we stopped acting like we could solve it alone.

That’s what 2025 clarified for us. Impact doesn’t scale through a single company, no matter how well it’s run. It scales through networks—B Corps, 1% for the Planet members, and companies that have decided impact is part of the operating system, not a side project.

We need more of them. A lot more.

This report isn’t a scoreboard. It’s a signal.

If you’re building a business and tired of choosing between growth and doing the right thing, there’s another way—through collective action, shared accountability, and a willingness to put numbers behind intent.

Because the world doesn’t need more statements about sustainability.

It needs more companies willing to carry it.

That’s the work ahead.

Our people · B Corp Certified

Becoming a Certified B Corporation™

B Corp Certification wasn't the goal. Accountability was.

Three years ago, we entered the B Corp process to measure ourselves against a higher standard—one that examines how a business treats its people, serves its communities, manages its environmental impact, and governs itself over the long term.

In August 2025, Booth became a Certified B Corporation™, joining a global community of businesses using profit as a tool for positive impact.

The certification reflects work happening across the company. But if there's one area where that commitment shows up every day, it's in our people.

From employee wellbeing and engagement to leadership representation and responsible purchasing practices, these are some of the ways we invest in the people who help build Booth.

How we invest in people

Engaged people do better work. We invest in the conditions that drive it — benefits, inclusion practices, and a culture built for the long haul.

What we track

Workforce data across gender, engagement, and development, benchmarked against industry standards. 20–29% of our workforce lives in low-income areas. That reality informs how and where we invest.

How we spend

40–59% of our procurement goes to local independent suppliers, and 10–24% to businesses majority-owned by women or underrepresented groups.

What's next

We're developing our JEDI (Justice, Equity, Diversity, and Inclusion) Data Collection Policy to better understand representation at every level of the organization, including more precise racial and ethnic data over time.

Looking ahead

The work isn't done.

Here's what we're committing to next.

01 · People

Advance JEDI (Justice, Equity, Diversity, and Inclusion) data collection — so we can understand and improve representation at every level of our organization

02 · Planet

Deepen environmental partnerships with MAD Earth, WWF, and Centre for Sustainability — moving from one-off activities toward sustained, year-over-year conservation commitments

03 · Forests

Reach 10,000 Almaciga seedlings and advocate for Sultan's Peak — a biodiversity-rich forest in the Philippines — to receive official government protection as a National Forest

04 · Community

Expand the ARK Solves partnership — scale food security programming beyond 122 communities

05 · Volunteers

Shift to departmental CSR — empowering each team to choose and lead their own causes, so our people engage with the work that matters most to them

06 · Reporting

Strengthen impact measurement — from ranges to exact numbers, comparable year over year

This is how we grow.

With accountability. With communities. And with more work ahead than behind us.
